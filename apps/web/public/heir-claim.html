1 · Your vault details

Your designator should have given you these (or a pre-filled link). The RPC is any public Polygon node — change it to one you trust if you like.

2 · What the result means

  • Inheritance fired — the dead-man’s switch (or an heir-claim) has activated on-chain. Your next-of-kin access is live; proceed to reconstruct the vault.
  • You hold an Activation / Voting / Revocation NFT — these soulbound tokens are your on-chain proof of role. Voting holders are exactly who an M-of-N heir quorum needs.
  • Not fired yet — the owner is still checking in (alive), or the switch has not yet been triggered. Nothing to claim until it fires.

3 · How to actually reconstruct the vault

The cryptography is fully client-side and needs no NoKLock server:

  1. If the NoKLock app is reachable, open the heir-restore page — drop your claim-envelope, the manifest, and the share files, then enter the master password your designator shared. For an M-of-N vault, use the “Form B unavailable? local heir quorum” panel: each heir signs locally and the app verifies the signatures against the on-chain Voting holders — again, no NoKLock server.
  2. If the NoKLock app is gone too, the restore is still pure Shamir + AEAD: any wallet/crypto engineer can recombine the threshold of share files and decrypt with the master password using the open manifest. The manifest documents the scheme (SLIP-39 / Shamir threshold + per-share AEAD).
  3. The share files could be local folders, a USB stick, or cloud links — wherever your designator put them. You only need threshold-many, not all of them.

What you need

  • The master password your designator shared with you (out-of-band — not on this page).
  • At least the threshold number of share files.
  • (M-of-N vaults) M heir wallets willing to sign — their addresses are the on-chain Voting holders this page can confirm.
